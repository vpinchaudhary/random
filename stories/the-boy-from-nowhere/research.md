## IPL Facts (CONFIRMED)

### IPL Season 1 — 2008
- Franchise auction: January 24, 2008
- Season started: **April 2008**
- **8 teams**: Chennai Super Kings (CSK), Mumbai Indians (MI), Delhi Daredevils (DD), Kings XI Punjab (KXIP), Deccan Chargers (DC), Rajasthan Royals (RR), Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR), Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB)
- **Mumbai Indians owned by**: Mukesh Ambani / Reliance Industries — most expensive franchise at $111.9 million
- **Icon Player**: Sachin Tendulkar (did not enter auction, signed directly)
- Salary cap: $5 million per team, minimum spend $3.3 million
- MS Dhoni was most expensive player: $1.5 million to CSK
- Shane Warne bought by Rajasthan Royals for $450,000
- **Winner**: Rajasthan Royals (Shane Warne's team — the underdogs won)
- Under-19 draft: snake format, fixed salaries ~$30,000-$50,000
- Virat Kohli entered IPL through Under-19 draft to RCB
- Pakistani players featured ONLY in Season 1 (excluded after Nov 2008 Mumbai attacks)
- **Mumbai Indians signed Ajinkya Rahane** as a young talent in 2008

### Realistic Path for an 18-year-old to IPL 2008
- Under-19 Player Draft: 16 uncapped players, mostly from India's U-19 World Cup squad
- Catchment area signings: Mumbai-based talent signed directly by MI
- Could be discovered through: Mumbai age-group cricket → U-19 performances → caught MI scouts' attention → signed via catchment area or auction

### Under-19 Cricket Pathway
- BCCI organizes: U-14, U-16, U-19, U-25 tournaments
- Typical path: school cricket → club cricket → district selection → state age-group team → Ranji Trophy squad
- Mumbai has the deepest talent pool and most competitive selection process in India
- Record 42 Ranji Trophy titles

### Key Mumbai Cricket Locations
- **Shivaji Park, Dadar**: The cathedral of Mumbai cricket. Open maidan, multiple matches simultaneously, where legends trained
- **Wankhede Stadium**: Mumbai's international ground, 33,000 capacity
- **Brabourne Stadium**: CCI ground, historic
- **Cross Maidan, Azad Maidan**: Open grounds in South Mumbai where club cricket happens
- **DY Patil Stadium**: Navi Mumbai, used for IPL

### Mumbai Cricketing Culture
- "Khadoos" spirit: stubborn, tenacious, never-give-up batting mentality
- Famous players from humble backgrounds: Sachin Tendulkar (middle-class Dadar), Vinod Kambli (working class), Ajinkya Rahane
- Gully cricket → local clubs → district → state pathway is real and proven

### Mumbai 2000-2010
- **2007**: India wins T20 World Cup — T20 cricket explodes
- **2008 November 26**: Mumbai terror attacks (Taj Hotel, CST station) — devastating event
- Street kids: odd jobs, restaurant work, surviving on charm and hustle
- ISPL (street cricket league) later formalized what gully cricket talent always was

## Shraddha Kapoor Facts (CONFIRMED)

### Biography
- **Born**: March 3, 1987, Mumbai
- **Father**: Shakti Kapoor (born Sunil Kapoor, September 3, 1952, Delhi)
- **Mother**: Shivangi Kapoor (née Kolhapure) — classical singer
- **Brother**: Siddhanth Kapoor (actor)
- **Aunts**: Padmini Kolhapure and Tejaswini Kolhapure (actresses)
- **Great-niece of**: Lata Mangeshkar, Asha Bhosle (legendary singers)
- **Education**: Jamnabai Narsee School → American School of Bombay → Boston University (psychology, dropped out after 1 year)
- **Scored 95% in Class 12 boards** — academically bright
- **Personality**: Wanted to be a psychologist originally. Trained singer since childhood. Used to rehearse film dialogues wearing parents' clothes as a child.
- **First film offer**: Age 16, from Salman Khan for Lucky: No Time for Love (2005) — she REJECTED it
- **Acting debut**: Teen Patti (2010) alongside Amitabh Bachchan
- **Breakthrough**: Aashiqui 2 (2013)
- **Forbes India Celebrity 100** since 2014

### Shakti Kapoor
- Born Sunil Kapoor in Delhi, Punjabi Hindu family
- Father ran a clothes shop in Connaught Place
- Studied at FTII (Film and Television Institute of India)
- Spotted by Sunil Dutt while making Rocky (to launch Sanjay Dutt)
- Renamed from "Sunil" to "Shakti" for his villainous screen presence
- Known for villainous AND comic roles — over 700 films
- Famous role: Crime Master Gogo (Andaz Apna Apna)
- Personality: Larger than life, dramatic, protective of family
