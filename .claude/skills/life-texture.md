# Life Texture Injector

## Purpose
Given a chapter outline, generate 2-3 "life moments" — small slice-of-life scenes (200-500 words each in outline form) that sit between plot beats. These scenes exist because life happens, not because the plot needs them. They make the story feel lived-in, not constructed.

## When to Use
Place as dedicated interlude scenes or mini-chapters between plot chapters — NOT in every chapter. Add them every 4-6 chapters, or wherever the story needs breathing room. A slice-of-life moment earns its place when the story has been running hot (post-match, post-crisis, post-revelation) and the reader needs to see these people just being people before the next plot beat hits.

## What Life Texture IS
- Shraddha botching a haircut during COVID lockdown and posting it on Instagram
- Vipin and Kohli arguing about vegetarian protein in a team bus
- Vipin trying to cook and burning dal while on the phone with Rao
- A teammate stealing Vipin's rubber ball as a prank and the entire dressing room watching him try to act like he doesn't care
- Shraddha teaching Vipin to take selfies (he's terrible at it)
- Vipin discovering online shopping and buying 14 rubber balls in different colors
- Ravi drunk-calling Vipin after a loss and talking about their gully cricket days

## What Life Texture IS NOT
- Plot-advancing scenes disguised as slice-of-life
- Exposition delivered through casual conversation
- Scenes that exist to "show character growth" — they show character, period
- Generic "friends hanging out" — every moment must be SPECIFIC to these people

## Rules

### 1. Period Accuracy
- Reference real cultural moments: specific films, songs, memes, events from that year
- Technology must match the era (no smartphones in 2008, no Instagram before 2012)
- Cricket references must be real: actual matches, actual players, actual results

### 2. Character Specificity
- Every life moment must be something ONLY these characters would do
- Vipin counts things. Always. Even in casual moments.
- Shraddha arranges things by color. Sings unconsciously. Mind-reads emotions.
- Rao is dry, measured, speaks in cricket metaphors
- Ravi is loud, social, the bridge between worlds
- Real cricket players must behave like themselves (Kohli's intensity, Dhoni's calm, Bumrah's quiet focus)

### 3. Sensory Grounding
- Every life moment needs one specific physical detail that anchors it
- Smells, textures, sounds — not visual descriptions alone
- The detail should be mundane: the sound of a pressure cooker, the smell of Dettol, the texture of a cheap hotel towel

### 4. Anti-AI Compliance
- No neat resolutions within the moment
- Not everything is meaningful — some things just happen
- Characters can be boring, petty, silly, gross
- Real people don't always have the right words
- Conversations trail off, get interrupted, go nowhere

### 5. Real Player Integration
- When cricket teammates appear in life moments, they must be period-accurate
- MI squad composition must match the real year
- India teammates must be who was actually in the squad that year
- Players should have recognizable personality traits (not caricatures)

## Output Format
For each chapter, output:
1. **Where it goes**: Which existing scene it sits within or between
2. **The moment**: 200-500 word outline-style description
3. **Sensory anchor**: The one physical detail that grounds it
4. **Period marker**: What makes this moment belong to THIS year specifically

## External POV Moments
Some life texture moments should be from perspectives OTHER than Vipin's — scenes where he's not present:
- Selectors discussing him in a BCCI room
- Kapoor family conversations about "that boy"
- Rao alone, processing what Vipin has become
- Bhai watching Vipin on TV
- Opposition players discussing how to bowl to him
- Ravi talking about Vipin to his own family
- Anjali Mehta pitching stories about him to editors
- Groundskeeper's quiet observations
- Shraddha's friends asking her about the cricketer

These scenes make Vipin three-dimensional by showing how he exists in OTHER people's lives when he's not looking.
