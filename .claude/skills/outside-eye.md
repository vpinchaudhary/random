# Outside Eye

## Purpose
Generate standalone interlude scenes told from perspectives OTHER than Vipin's — scenes where he is the subject but not the narrator. These exist as dedicated interlude chapters or sections placed between plot chapters, NOT crammed into every chapter. They make the world feel real by showing Vipin's ripple effect on people he doesn't know he's affecting.

## When to Use
Place interludes SPARINGLY — after every 4-6 plot chapters, or at natural story inflection points (after a big match, a public moment, a controversy). Not every stretch of the story needs one. The rule: if the outside perspective reveals something the reader can't get from Vipin's POV, it earns its place. If it just repeats what we already know from a different angle, cut it.

## What Outside Eye IS
- A BCCI selector arguing Vipin's case over chai while his colleague dismisses "another street story"
- Shakti Kapoor sitting alone after meeting Vipin, turning over the gold pendant detail in his mind
- A club bowler Vipin destroyed in a match, now coaching kids, using "that Chaudhary innings" as a teaching moment years later
- Anjali Mehta's editor rejecting her Vipin story pitch — "nobody reads about uncapped players" — then calling her back after IPL debut
- Bhai watching Vipin on a TV in a chai shop, calculating what this boy's fame means for his own exposure
- Opposition captain's team meeting: "Don't bowl short to him. Don't sledge him. He feeds on it."
- Shraddha's college friends googling Vipin after she mentions him, finding nothing — "he doesn't even have a Facebook?"
- Sunita Devi in Haryana seeing a cricket match on a neighbor's TV and not recognizing her own grandson
- A random spectator at Wankhede telling his wife about "this kid from nowhere" on the drive home

## What Outside Eye IS NOT
- Vipin-worship from every angle (some people should be indifferent, dismissive, or wrong about him)
- Exposition dumps disguised as dialogue ("As you know, Vipin was found on temple steps...")
- Every outside observer reaching a profound realization — some just notice him and move on
- Balanced or fair perspectives every time — people are biased, jealous, wrong, or simply distracted

## Rules

### 1. No Vipin Present
He cannot be in the scene. He doesn't know these conversations happen. The reader gets privileged access to how the world processes him without his knowledge.

### 2. The Observer Has Their Own Life
The POV character is not a camera pointed at Vipin. They have their own problems, dinner getting cold, a kid crying in the next room, a deadline. Vipin is something that passes through their day, not the center of it (even when he's the topic).

### 3. Contradictory Views Welcome
Two selectors can disagree about Vipin. Shakti can be wrong about him. A journalist can miss the real story. A teammate can resent him. The outside eye doesn't confirm Vipin's greatness — it shows how differently one person can land in different minds.

### 4. Period and Context Accuracy
- Real cricket events, real cultural references, real technology for the year
- BCCI politics must reflect actual power structures of the era
- Media landscape must match (print dominance pre-2012, social media explosion post-2012, WhatsApp university post-2016)

### 5. Varying Scale
Not every outside eye is a major character. Some are:
- **Recurring**: Selectors, Anjali Mehta, Shakti, Bhai, Sunita Devi
- **One-time**: A taxi driver, a net bowler, a hotel receptionist, a rival's father
- **Institutional**: Committee meetings, editorial rooms, broadcast commentary boxes

## Placement Strategy
Interludes work best:
- **After Act breaks** (after Ch 10, between Act 2A/2B, after Act 3 crisis)
- **After public moments** (IPL debut, media story breaks, relationship goes public, scandal)
- **Before time jumps** (the interlude bridges the gap and shows what happened in the world while the story skipped ahead)
- **During Vipin's absences** (injury recovery, being dropped, separation from Shraddha)

Interludes should NOT appear:
- During high-momentum plot sequences (don't interrupt a cricket match or crisis)
- Back to back (never two interludes in a row)
- When the reader already has the information from another angle

## Output Format
1. **Placement**: Where in the chapter sequence this interlude sits (e.g., "Between Ch 14 and Ch 15")
2. **POV Character**: Who sees, with one line on their own situation
3. **The Scene**: 300-800 word outline-style description
4. **What the Reader Learns**: The one thing this scene gives that Vipin's POV cannot
5. **Sensory anchor**: One grounding physical detail from the observer's world (not Vipin's)
